# Research

Our lab's research spans **Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence & Neural Networks, Data Science, and Data Mining**, with a particular focus on applying these methods to healthcare, human-signal analysis, and financial data. Below are the main threads of our ongoing and past work.

### Medical Image Analysis & Healthcare AI
We develop machine learning pipelines for automated disease detection and classification from medical images. This includes a skin-disease classification model that combines Grab-Cut segmentation with GLCM texture features, achieving 95.68% accuracy, and a K-means-based brain tumor segmentation and classification system reaching 92.38% accuracy. We also design CNN and MobileNetV2-based models for tasks such as face-mask detection, trained and validated on large public datasets with over 98% validation accuracy.

### Speech & Signal-Based Emotion Recognition
We study machine learning approaches to speech emotion recognition, benchmarking algorithms across diverse datasets to understand which features and models generalize best across languages and recording conditions, achieving up to 99% average classification accuracy in controlled evaluations.

### AI for Finance and Data Mining
We apply machine learning to structured financial data, exploring the value-relevance of accounting information for predicting market performance, and more broadly investigate data mining techniques for extracting actionable insight from real-world tabular datasets.

### Applied AI Systems
Beyond core ML research, the lab is interested in practical, deployable AI and IoT/robotics systems — reflecting our home department's focus on the Internet of Things and Robotics Engineering — including embedded sensing, automation, and intelligent decision-making systems.
